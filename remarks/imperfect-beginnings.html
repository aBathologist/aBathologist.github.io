Imperfect Beginnings

<2016-05-23 Mon>

I want a perfect start: I want a perfect initial post about a perfectly chosen topic to take shape in a perfectly designed site running on a perfectly configured implementation of a perfectly crafted framework. I want this improbably auspicious beginning because I want to avoid embarrassment, because I want to avoid confronting the meagerness of my abilities, and because a preoccupation with interminable parameter-fiddling and configuration-setting postpones the harder task of crafting content. Similarly, it is easier to write about how hard it is to start writing than it is to start writing meaningful things. But once one has begun that easier bit, a beginning has been made all the same!

perfect (adj.):

early 15c. alteration of Middle English parfit (c. 1300), from Old French parfit "finished, completed, ready" (11c.), from Latin perfectus "completed, excellent, accomplished, exquisite," past participle of perficere "accomplish, finish, complete," from per- "completely" (see per) + facere "to make, do, perform" (see factitious).

— etymonline.com

According to the etyma of 'perfect' a "perfect beginning" is an oxymoron. But our words don't mean what philologists think their roots meant millennia ago: everybody knows a perfect beginning is an optimal starting condition; no one thinks it is the paradox of an initiation simultaneously completing itself.

I have always had too much trouble getting projects started, and even more trouble keeping them underway. However, I am finally learning that any starting conditions that will enable me to move forward are good enough (thanks Andrew and Kat!). So this thing here—be it blog, website, notebook, or something else—is starting as a sketchy skeleton, barely formed, and maybe even malformed, but it is underway all the same!

A reader who follows the development of this space will see it gradually take shape, steadily changing form until it reaches its inevitably imperfect final state. I can safely say, perfection will be found neither at the beginning nor at the end, but maybe some bits and fragments will seem to find a perfect fit along the way.
